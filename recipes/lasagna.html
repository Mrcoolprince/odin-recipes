Lasagna

Home

Lasagna is a delicious Italian dish made with layers of flat pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy cheese, and sometimes vegetables.
Each layer is stacked neatly, usually topped with a generous amount of melted cheese that becomes golden and bubbly when baked.
The flavors blend together perfectly, creating a warm and hearty meal that many people enjoy for dinner or special gatherings.

The traditional lasagna recipe uses a meat-based sauce like Bolognese and a white sauce called béchamel, but there are many variations.
Some versions are made with spinach, mushrooms, or even seafood.
Whether made with meat or vegetables, lasagna is loved for its comforting taste and satisfying layers.
It's often served hot and pairs well with a simple salad or garlic bread.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 pound ground beef or sausage (optional)
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. If using meat, brown it in a skillet over medium heat. Drain excess fat.
  4. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, egg, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  5. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  6. Layer 3 noodles, half of the ricotta mixture, half of the meat (if using), and a third of the mozzarella cheese.
  7. Repeat layers, finishing with noodles topped with marinara sauce and remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  8. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes or until cheese is bubbly and golden.
  9. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil if desired.
  10. Enjoy your delicious homemade lasagna!
